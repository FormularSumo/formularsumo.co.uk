It's said that there's nothing like a good book - a physical book. But nowadays there's much more variety in how we read, and it would be wrong to say that reading on paper is always better, as each medium has its own advantages and drawbacks. We're currently living through a truly revolutionally period in the history of reading, and I'd like to give a balanced view of this situation, and offer a vision for the future.

The 3 main ways of reading are through physical object, digital displays, and orally. There's a lot of variety within these categories: an ebook vs a blog, or a school textbook vs a bestselling novel, but it's the easiest way of categorising them. I do a mixture of them myself, and I believe they all have their place, although it's true that they can be more appropriate in certain situations or for specific people.

Let's start with reading digitally. I think this is the least prestigious category, and I can definitely understand why.
